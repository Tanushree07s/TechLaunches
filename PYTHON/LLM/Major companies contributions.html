• Key Models: GPT-2, GPT-3, GPT-4, ChatGPT
  • Contributions:
    • Introduced the GPT family of large language models to that process and generate human-like text.
    • Made LLMs more usable by the public through ChatGPT.
    • Used a technique called Reinforcement Learning from Human Feedback (RLHF) This means people rated the model’s responses, and the model learned to give better answers over time.
    • Partnered with Microsoft to bring LLMs to Azure and Microsoft 365.

  • Key Models: BERT, PaLM, Gemini (formerly Bard)
  • Contributions:
    • BERT (2018) introduced bidirectional attention, which means the model looks at words before and after a blank in a sentence to understand context better (like solving: “The cat ___ on the mat” by looking at both sides).
    • PaLM showed how bigger models can learn new tasks with just a few examples ( few-shot learning ).
    • Gemini is their latest chatbot, similar to ChatGPT, built to work across text, images, and more.

  • Key Models: LLaMA models, OPT
  • Contributions:
    • Focused on open-weight models LLMs whose weight parameters are publicly accessible and can be modified without restriction,so researchers and companies can download, run, study, or fine-tune these models freely. with LLaMA (Large Language Model Meta AI) .
    • Made advanced LLMs more available to researchers, developers, and organizations beyond major tech firms.
    • Before LLaMA, Meta introduced the OPT (Open Pretrained Transformer) A family of open-source GPT-style language models released with complete training code and datasets to promote transparent research and enable easy comparison with other large models. .

  • Key Models: Claude models
  • Contributions:
    • Focused on building AI that is safe, honest, and helpful by reducing harmful or biased outputs. Anthropic emphasized alignment — training models to follow human values and respond appropriately in real-world use.
    • Created the Claude series, models known for their ability to reason carefully, follow instructions faithfully, and stay aligned with user intent.
    • Introduced Constitutional AI, a new training approach where the model is guided by a written set of principles or rules (like a constitution). Instead of only learning from human feedback, the model is taught to follow these values when answering — leading to more consistent, ethical, and safer responses without needing as much manual oversight.
